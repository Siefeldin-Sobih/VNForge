The classroom was empty except for the two of them. Everyone else had left after the bell, chairs still crooked from the rush. Soft afternoon light came through the half-open blinds and striped the floor in gold.

Hana had stayed behind to return a notebook she had borrowed two weeks ago. She had told herself that was the only reason. But when she placed it on Kenji's desk and he looked up from his book, she felt her rehearsed words dissolve completely.

He didn't say anything at first. He just looked at her like he was waiting — like he already knew something she hadn't said yet.

"I've been meaning to talk to you," Hana started. Her voice came out quieter than she intended. "About the culture festival. About what happened after."

Kenji closed his book slowly. Outside, the school grounds were filling with the noise of clubs and footsteps. In here, it was just the hum of the ventilation and the sound of her own heartbeat.

He stood up. One step toward her. "I remember what happened after," he said.
