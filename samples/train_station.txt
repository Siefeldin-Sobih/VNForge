Mia stood at the edge of the platform, her suitcase beside her. The last train of the night was three minutes away. She hadn't told anyone she was leaving — not her mother, not her roommate, and especially not Daniel.

Behind her, she heard footsteps. Fast ones. She turned and saw him, out of breath, his jacket half-buttoned like he had run the whole way from campus. He stopped a few feet away, hands at his sides, unsure what to say.

The wind pulled at her scarf. A few other passengers stood further down the platform, pretending not to notice.

Daniel took one step closer. "You weren't going to say goodbye?"

Mia looked at her suitcase, then back at him. The train horn sounded in the distance — still far, but coming. She had maybe two minutes to say something, or say nothing, or walk away before he could stop her.
