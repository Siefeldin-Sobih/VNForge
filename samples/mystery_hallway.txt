The hallway on the third floor had been locked since the accident. Everyone at Aldenmoor Academy knew that. The rusted chain on the stairwell door was supposed to be a permanent fixture — a reminder, or maybe a warning.

So when Rowan found the chain lying on the floor, unclipped and coiled like something discarded in a hurry, they stopped walking.

The hallway beyond the door was dark. The overhead lights had been removed, not burned out — removed. Rowan could see the empty sockets spaced down the ceiling like missing teeth. At the far end, a door stood slightly open. A thin line of pale light leaked through the gap.

There was a smell too. Something old. Like paper left in rain.

Rowan pulled out their phone and turned on the flashlight. The beam cut into the dark. On the wall to the left, written in something that had long since dried brown, were six words:

SHE DIDN'T FALL. SHE WAS MOVED.

Somewhere deeper in the hallway, a door clicked shut.
